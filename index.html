Mário Antunes

PhD in computer science from MAPi and Researcher in IT, Aveiro.

I received my Computer and Telematics engineering M.Sc. degree in 2011, with first-class honours, from the Electronics, Telecommunication and Informatics Department, University of Aveiro, Portugal. And concluded my doctoral program in computer science from MAPi in 2018. I'm currently working as a researcher for the 5G-Contact project at IT-Aveiro.

My main research areas focus on Knowledge Extraction and Context Storage in the Internet of Things (IoT) Scenarios using Machine Learning techniques and Big Data repositories. My works include developing efficient ways to deal with unstructured information. These techniques are being implemented and evaluated in advance machine-to-machine projects, such as APOLLO, SCOT and SGH.

My PhD in Computer Science was developed at University of Aveiro (UA) within the MAP-Tele Doctoral Program, December 2018, supervised by Diogo Gomes and Rui Aguiar (both from IT/UA).

The thesis “Knowledge extraction from semi-structured sources” addresses the problem of storing and organizing context information from IoT/M2M scenarios in a meaningful way, without imposing a representation scheme or requiring a priori relations. This work proposes a d-dimension organization model, which was optimized for IoT/M2M data. During this work, two different machine learning techniques were tackled: semantic and stream similarity.
